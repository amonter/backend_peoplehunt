Galway Actor's Workshop has been set up to open the experience of drama and acting to the people of Galway in a variety of self-contained workshops and short term courses.

We believe that for all of you have ever just wanted to give it a try, it is never too late! While providing you with solid training at whatever level you choose, we also hope to enable you to experience the sense of fun and personal creativity that comes from engaging with drama.

0ur expert Sarah O'Toole is a former member of Galway Youth Theatre and a graduate of the Gaiety School of Acting. She worked in Dublin as an actor, playing parts in the Gate's production of Pride and Prejudice, The Machine's Mysteries 2000 as well as appearing in the films Disco Pigs and Fair City.

She then went on to train as a director in Middlesex University's International MA in Theatre Directing course where she specialised in Shakespeare, Comedy, Michael Chekhov and Balinese Theatre. As part of her studies she spent time training at the Russian Academy of Theatre Arts in Moscow and ISI in Denpasar, Bali.

She then moved to Glasgow where she played several major parts including Jane Eyre and Anna Karenina, as well as setting up her own theatre company and Improv Troupe, Razed Curtain.

She is delighted to be back in Galway and to share her love of theatre with the people of her hometown!

Here are the answers for the inquiring minds:
Who is it for?
This is for people from all age groups and genders, who would like to keep developing their social skills. Social skills are in a way like exercising our bodies. When you stop, it becomes harder and more painful to get back to you previous level. On the other hand when you do it constantly, it becomes a fun and challenging activity and you become better at it.
How is it going to work?
This workshop is planned to be 3 hrs long. The first half will be spend on theory (building confidence, conversational skills, social anxiety and group dynamics). The other half will be spend talking to people that you don’t know and get some real life practice.
What are the benefits of talking to Strangers?
Well, I can say that the benefits are just far too great. Universities and schools never teach us on how to talk to people we have never met before. We have developed our own social skills from friends, books, emulation or simply by instinct. The benefits involved gaining a big amount of personal confidence and a more fulfilling social life. Talking to people you don’t know could easily develop into business connections, networking, meeting new interesting people or simply romance.
Who is giving this workshop?
My name is Adrian. I am the founder of meetforeal.com and from my personal experience. I have learned so much from talking to strangers. In the past 7 months I have spoken to more than 150 new strangers from all regions and nationalities. I have lived in 4 different countries and I speak 3 different languages. I have a Masters degree on Computer Science believe it or not. I have learned so much from the actual practice and theory that is so much fun. I would like to share all what I have learned.
Is it not something you learn anyway?
Yes and no at the same time. People usually don’t feel very comfortable talking to people they don’t know very well. How about setting a specific goal to talk to a certain amount of people you don’t know? This will give you loads of stamina to grow and maintain your social skills muscles. Gradually you will feel more confident and comfortable talking to strangers.
How much does it cost?
Its 25 euro for the whole workshop. Please if you are interested send me an email adrian@meetforeal.com,or add it to your events!
